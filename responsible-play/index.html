Responsible Play

ThaiLotteryNumbers.com is an informational and educational resource. We publish official Government Lottery Office (GLO) results, historical records, and statistics so the public can look up and understand draws that have already happened. We are not a gambling operator, we do not sell tickets, and nothing on this site is a prediction or a recommendation to play.

The lottery is entertainment, not an investment

The Thai Government Lottery is a game of chance. Every draw is independent and random. No pattern, “hot” number, or historical frequency changes the odds of any number being drawn next — and anyone claiming to predict winning numbers is mistaken or misleading you. Treat any money spent on tickets as the cost of entertainment, never as a way to make money or solve financial problems.

Play within your means

  • Only ever spend money you can comfortably afford to lose.
  • Set a personal limit before you buy, and never chase losses.
  • Lottery spending should never come before essentials, savings, or family needs.
  • Buying more tickets does not meaningfully improve your odds of the top prize.

Understanding the odds

Matching all six digits for the first prize is extremely unlikely on any single ticket. We explain the real probabilities in plain language in our guides so you can make informed, realistic decisions. Understanding the odds is the single best protection against unrealistic expectations.

Protecting yourself and others

Gambling should stay fun. If it stops being fun — if you feel you can’t stop, if you’re spending more than you intended, hiding it, or borrowing to play — that is a sign to seek support. These feelings are common and help is available.

Where to get help (Thailand)

If you or someone you know may have a gambling problem, you can talk to a professional in confidence. In Thailand, the Department of Mental Health hotline 1323 offers free, 24-hour mental-health support. Your doctor or a local hospital can also refer you to counselling services.

(If you are outside Thailand, please seek a local problem-gambling support service in your country.)
